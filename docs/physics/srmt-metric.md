# SRMT affine-fit quality `q` тАФ derivation and alternatives

## Statement of the conjecture under test

The Superspace-Relational Modular Time (SRMT) conjecture in this project's
WheelerтАУDeWitt setting asserts: for a bipartition of the minisuperspace
wavefunction `╧З(a, ╧ЖтВБ, ╧ЖтВВ)` along the DeWitt-timelike scale-factor axis
`a`, the **modular Hamiltonian** `K_A = тИТlog ╧Б_A` of the reduced density
matrix on one side of the cut is unitarily equivalent, on the low-lying
spectrum, to the **HamiltonтАУJacobi (HJ) operator** generated by
`U(a, ╧Ж) = a ┬╖ V(╧Ж) тИТ a`, evaluated on the slice orthogonal to the cut.

That is, let `{s_n}` be the Schmidt singular values across the cut and

```text
K_n := тИТlog(s_n┬▓ + ╬╡),        n = 0, 1, тАж, rтИТ1
```

be the modular spectrum (ascending), and let `{E_n}` be the leading `r`
eigenvalues of the discrete HJ operator on the slice. The strict SRMT
conjecture is

```text
(C1)     K_n = E_n + c,     c constant in n,
```

for clock `a` but **not** for clocks `╧ЖтВБ, ╧ЖтВВ`. `c` absorbs the
zero-of-energy convention (HJ eigenvalues are defined up to an additive
constant; modular `K` is defined up to the trace normalisation `tr ╧Б = 1`,
which fixes the arithmetic mean of `K_n` but not its absolute zero).

## The affine-fit metric `q_affine`

The live diagnostic (`src/lib/physics/srmt/affineFit.ts`) reports

```text
q_affine = ╬г_n (K_n тИТ (╬▒┬╖E_n + ╬▓))┬▓ / ╬г_n K_n┬▓                      (1)
```

where `(╬▒, ╬▓)` are the linear-least-squares fit minimisers across the
first `rankCap` modes. The residual is dimensionless and scale-invariant
in `E` (╬▒ absorbs scale). It is **not** generally shift-invariant in `K`:
while ╬▓ removes shifts from the numerator, the denominator `╬г K_n┬▓` is
not shift-invariant, so translating `K` changes `q` when the fit is
imperfect.

`╬▓` is the clock's zero-of-energy offset тАФ the constant term the HJ
spectrum is defined up to. Under the **volume-weighted** Schmidt
normalisation `╬г s_n┬▓ ┬╖ dVol = 1` (task #8, implemented in
`src/lib/physics/srmt/schmidt.ts:normalizedSchmidtValues`), `╬▓` tracks
only the genuine physics offset: neither `тИТlog(╬г|╧З|┬▓)` (the raw-buffer
artefact that produced the historical `╬▓ тЙИ тИТ14`) nor `log(dVol)` (the
Frobenius-only residual that drifted across `gridNa` / `gridNphi` /
`phiExtent` sweeps) leaks in. Expected magnitude `|╬▓| тЙ▓ 1` at any
well-posed physics configuration; a reported `|╬▓| > 5` in a production
sweep signals a diagnostic-pipeline bug, not physics.

`q_affine = 0` iff `K_n = ╬▒┬╖E_n + ╬▓` for some `(╬▒, ╬▓)`. This tests a
**strictly weaker** claim than (C1):

```text
(C2)     K_n = ╬▒┬╖E_n + ╬▓,     ╬▒ > 0, ╬▓ тИИ тДЭ.
```

Under (C2) the two spectra are affinely related тАФ the same linear order
тАФ but not numerically identical. A universe where (C1) fails but (C2)
holds is one where clock `a` is an affine image of HJ: the same physics,
up to a rescaling of time. Whether that counts as "SRMT holds" depends on
how strictly one reads the conjecture. **This project's position: `q_affine`
alone is not sufficient evidence for (C1).**

### Why ╬▒-freedom matters

The HJ operator is built on a discretised PDE whose leading eigenvalues
scale with `1/╬Фx┬▓`. The modular `K_n` are log-singular values of a
finite-dimensional Schmidt decomposition тАФ no PDE discretisation appears.
Equating `K` and `E` *numerically* requires matching units, which in turn
requires a choice of `тДП` scaling, grid spacing convention, and a factor
that converts between canonical pair variables. Absorbing this conversion
into an unconstrained `╬▒` is exactly what the affine fit does.

The price: `q_affine` can reward a theory where the two spectra have the
same *shape* but disagree on *absolute magnitude* by an order of
magnitude тАФ equivalent, numerically, to "clock `a` runs at a different
rate than HJ but is otherwise HJ". That is not the conjecture.

## The rigid-fit metric `q_rigid`

Fix ╬▒ = 1 and minimise over ╬▓ only:

```text
╬▓* = mean_n K_n тИТ mean_n E_n                                        (2)
q_rigid = ╬г_n (K_n тИТ E_n тИТ ╬▓*)┬▓ / ╬г_n K_n┬▓                          (3)
```

This is the test of (C1) directly: ╬▓ absorbs the zero-of-energy, ╬▒ is
**not** free. By construction

```text
q_rigid тЙе q_affine,                                                  (4)
```

with equality iff the affine fit already chose ╬▒ = 1. The gap
`q_rigid тИТ q_affine` is a direct measure of how much the affine fit was
exploiting ╬▒ to lower the residual тАФ i.e. how much scale disagreement
between `K` and `E` the affine fit is hiding.

### Recommended reporting

Both metrics are computed per sweep point and emitted in the CSV export.
The plot still shows `q_affine` because it has the cleaner interpretation
("same spectral order") and is the number Sec. 5 of the thesis will cite.
But the CSV carries `q_rigid` alongside, and any numerical claim that
relies on the modularтАУHJ match MUST be checked against both:

- `q_rigid тЙк 1` is strong evidence for (C1).
- `q_affine тЙк 1 тИз q_rigid тЙ│ 1` is evidence for (C2) only тАФ the weaker
  claim.
- `q_rigid / q_affine > 10` flags a large ╬▒-dependence, which in turn
  flags a unit / scale mismatch that must be understood before reading
  the result as "SRMT holds".
